OUR RECENT ADDITIONS

Artist: Daniel Van der Vaart.

Medium: Cement,steel(wires)

Title: Pink tales

Year: 2020

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION: This artwork illustrates the artists time in the Netherlands. Growing up in a home sheltering 5 kids and parents working day and night to keep their bellies full, Daniel as the eldest child was responsible for the kids. The hands represents the self sacrifice Daniel went through for his family. The hands are shown tearing the the main body apart. The colour of the sculpture(pink/grey) represents the innocence and youth in both the hands and the body to show that juat because the body is falling apart does not mean anybody had any ill intent.

Artist :Chen Xiu

Medium:

Title :Outside In Series: Swimming

Year :2020

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:Chen Xiu worked tirelessly for months to capture this specific aspect in her life. The outside in series (although only 1 of the artworks is available on the website) is various pure white, expresionless face sculptures that try to illustrate how the chinese people have to keep themselves emotionally shackled to survive. The swimming face sculpture is specifically to show how the peoples individualism is drowning from the trying too hard to fit in.

Artist : Olivier Lloris

Medium :Clay,Steel(wires)

Title :New world

Year :2019

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:From the beloved city of Paris Olivier wanted to show us the depths of its dark side that most people do not see from a far. The sculptures represent the citizens of France that endures through the crime. The few sculptures with no face represent those who do not endure the crime but still ignore it when seeing it happen to others. These sculptures amazingly capture the expressions Olivier wanted to convey.

Artist :Alvaro Torres

Medium :Metal (human figures),Wood(board/stand)

Title :Society

Year :2020

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:Alvaros work captures the joys and loneliness we have to deal with in reality. Some figures look frustrated or depressed when alone but there are those that still look happy in solitude.The white background gives excellent emphasis on the figures even if they are small in size. The figures with companions all look happy.This artwork shows how human beings really are social creatures.
